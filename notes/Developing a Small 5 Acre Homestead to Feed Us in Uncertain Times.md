## [[NOTES]]

## Water System

- A blockage in the water supply was identified and cleared — shown at the opening of the video
- Abundant winter rains had a positive effect on the land and plant recovery

## Olive Tree Pruning

- Harriet had been pruning olive trees, acknowledging being slightly late in the season
- Last year was the **first major structural prune** after years of neglect — trees were unproductive as a result
- This year's pruning is lighter: removing fewer branches, refining shape, and encouraging healthy productive trees
- Olive prunings are fed to the donkeys

## Asparagus Bed Establishment

- Asparagus is a **long-term perennial crop**: stays in the same place for 5, 10, or even 15 years
- **First harvest not expected until 2028**
- Planting method:
    - Soil aerated and amended with sand (asparagus requires loose structure and good drainage)
    - Compost and manure dug in to enrich the ground for long-term nutrient supply
    - Crowns planted approximately **30 cm apart**
    - Root tips trimmed and spread into a **star shape** before planting

> [!important] Intercropping Strategy
> While waiting for asparagus to establish, the bed space is used productively:
>
> - **Strawberries** planted in the asparagus bed (shallow roots — no competition)
>     
> - **Lettuces** planted along back row (shade-tolerant, placed in shadow cast by straw bales)
>     
> - **Radishes** planted along the front row (fast-growing, before heat sets in)
>     

## Straw Bale Ground Preparation Method

- Straw bales left on the ground for **4–6 months** clear vegetation underneath
- Advantage over plastic: does **not bake the ground** in hot climates — soil remains soft, spongy, moist, and alive when bales are removed
- Disadvantage: slow process, limited area at a time (bales are small)
- Workflow:
    
    1. Place bales on target area
    2. After 4–6 months, remove bales — move to the edge of the newly prepared bed
    3. Bales continue to suppress weeds at the edge
    4. When fully rotted, use as mulch; if still intact, move and repeat elsewhere
        
- Compost added to beds after preparation

> [!warning] Plastic Mulch in Hot Climates
> Plastic bakes the soil surface in hot, dry climates like Castellón. This destroys soil biology. Straw bales are the preferred alternative.

## Raised Bed Construction Approach

- Harriet builds raised bed borders using **logs** (not treated lumber — too expensive)
- Barter arrangement: gives friends vegetable boxes in exchange for logs from their pine thinning work
- Grass and encroachment from the wet winter is a persistent challenge
- Plans to convert the tilled area progressively into raised beds for better management
- Chicken tractor used to manage back edges; too narrow for some paths

## Potato Planting

- Variety planted: **Alouette** — purple-skinned with yellow flesh

> [!note] Determinate vs. Indeterminate Potatoes
>
> - **Determinate (e.g., Alouette)**: produces tubers in one layer just below the soil surface; does not keep producing from the stem; does **not** require heavy hilling
>     
> - **Indeterminate**: continues producing tubers up the stem; requires repeated hilling
>     

- Planting data:
    - **3 kg of seed potatoes = 55 plants** in an 18 m bed
    - Spacing: approximately **30–32 cm between plants**
    - Seed potatoes planted whole (not cut into segments) due to large volume ordered
    - Total of **5 trays** of seed potatoes this season
    - Cover with soil in trench; add straw around stems as they grow to keep tubers from light
    - No further hilling required for this determinate variety
- Strategy: planted a **mix of early and main crop varieties** to spread the harvest and reduce storage burden
- Realisation mid-planting: needed to expand the garden area; used a **rotivator** to prepare additional space
- Estimate of 280 plants total against a recommended 10–20 per person (family significantly over-planted)

## Almond Terrace Restoration

- Mauro worked on clearing dead almond trees from the terrace
- Dead tree identification criteria: **no green sprouting during a peak-water year** = likely dead; confirmed by hollow or completely dry interior when cut
- Work took several full mornings
- Chainsaw chain wore down completely; considering upgrading to a **diamond chain** (noted as expensive)
- The terrace originally held **30–40 almond trees**; only approximately **10 remain alive**
- Observations on terrace layout: almonds on lower terrace, olives in a row, carob in the row above

> [!important] Terrace Management Plan
>
> - Re-fence the top terrace to exclude donkeys from the bottom terrace
>     
> - Donkeys can be let in occasionally to graze when grass is plentiful
>     
> - Protect the historic dry stone terrace wall (4–5 m tall), which has deteriorated due to donkey traffic
>     
> - Plant young almond trees to restore the terrace to original density
>     
> - Clear overgrown section first with strimmer and mini chainsaw before planting
>     

## Philosophy of Food Self-Sufficiency

> [!quote] Direct Statement from Video

## [[KEY TAKEAWAYS]]

1. **Straw bales are superior to plastic mulch in hot, dry climates** — they clear ground without baking and destroying soil biology, and transition into mulch when fully decomposed.
2. **Asparagus requires heavy soil preparation upfront** — sand for drainage, compost and manure for long-term fertility — because the crown will occupy the same ground for 10–15 years.
3. **Intercropping the asparagus bed** with strawberries, lettuce, and radishes maximises yield from ground that will not produce asparagus until 2028.
4. **Determinate potato varieties (like Alouette) do not require heavy hilling** — cover in the trench and add straw around stems as they grow; no mounding needed.
5. **Planting a mix of early and main crop potato varieties** distributes the harvest over time and reduces the burden of single-point storage.
6. **Dead almond trees in a peak-water year** (confirmed dead if no green growth and hollow/dry interior) can be cleared to make space for replanting.
7. **Excluding livestock from tree terraces is essential** when replanting young trees and protecting historic dry stone walls from structural damage.
8. **Food self-sufficiency has evolved beyond lifestyle choice** — it is framed as a community and civilisational necessity in response to global uncertainty.
9. **Barter economies (vegetables for logs)** provide access to materials that would otherwise be prohibitively expensive.
10. **Seasonal timing matters**: olive pruning, radish sowing, and corn preparation all have narrow windows that come around faster than expected.

## F. FAQ

> [!question] Q: Why use straw bales instead of black plastic to suppress weeds?
> **A:** In hot, dry climates like Castellón, black plastic bakes the soil, destroying soil biology and leaving the ground hard and lifeless. Straw bales cool and protect the soil, leaving it soft, moist, and biologically active when removed.

> [!question] Q: Why won't Alouette potatoes be harvested at different depths?
> **A:** Alouette is a determinate variety. It produces all its tubers in a single horizontal layer just below the soil surface and does not continue building tubers up the stem as it grows. Indeterminate varieties do this, hence requiring repeated hilling.

> [!question] Q: Why plant asparagus crowns in sand?
> **A:** Asparagus requires well-drained, loose soil structure. Compacted or waterlogged soil leads to crown rot. Sand improves drainage and aeration around the roots.

> [!question] Q: Why not cut seed potatoes into segments?
> **A:** Theoretically it increases plant numbers per kilo. However, in this case, the volume of seed potatoes ordered was already high (5 trays), so whole planting was chosen to avoid overproduction and ensure adequate space.

> [!question] Q: Why are donkeys being excluded from the almond terrace?
> **A:** Young almond trees need protection from trampling. Donkeys also damage the historic dry stone terrace wall by moving up and down the slope. Controlled, occasional grazing access is acceptable once the trees are established.

## Portfolio & Project Ideas

- **Weekend project**: establish a single straw bale ground clearing experiment in a small garden area; document soil condition before and after
- **Seasonal project**: plant an asparagus bed with intercropped radishes and lettuces; track yields from interim crops vs. long-term asparagus production timeline
- **Multi-year project**: document a full potato season — planting mixed early/main crop varieties, photographing each variety at harvest, recording yields per bed

## Learning Path Connections

- **Prerequisites**: basic soil science, understanding of perennial vs. annual plant cycles, irrigation fundamentals
- **Next steps**: composting systems, seed saving, food preservation (fermentation, canning), agroforestry design
- **Related topics**: companion planting, cover cropping, no-dig gardening, integrated pest management

## Pro Tips

> [!tip] Pro Tip
> When establishing perennial beds (asparagus, strawberries, fruit trees), invest heavily in soil preparation before planting — you will not have easy access to the root zone for a decade. One thorough preparation is worth ten years of top-dressing.

> [!tip] Pro Tip
> Plant early and main crop potato varieties in the same season. Early varieties free up bed space faster, and main crops store longer. This staggers harvest pressure and storage demand simultaneously.

## 语感 / 用法值得注意的点（nuances）

- “bake the ground” 不是字面烤地，而是比喻塑料覆盖+强烈阳光让土壤过热、失去生命力的效果。​
- “spongy, moist, alive” 一连串形容词，既是字面质地描述，也是“土壤有生命”的隐喻式说法，语气很自然生活化。​
- “things have gone a little bit awry” 是一种温和幽默的自我吐槽，不是灾难级，而是“排版、规划有点乱套”。​
- “take a backseat” 常用来说某个目标 / 事情暂时退居二线，被更紧急的事优先。​
- “shift more into the center stage” 直接借舞台隐喻，表示“成为核心焦点”。​
- “in these uncertain times” 是当代英语里非常高频的时代情绪表达，用来承接对未来不确定、风险升高的背景。​
- “support plants” 在园艺里有具体含义（固氮、招益虫、遮阴、提供结构等），不是只是“支持一下”的泛泛说。​
