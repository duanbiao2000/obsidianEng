### C2 迷你作文式回答

> Absolutely, though I should say my interest comes in waves rather than being a constant thing. There are periods where I'll go down a bit of a rabbit hole — say, stumbling across a recipe video and suddenly deciding I need to master that particular dish, which then leads me to research the technique, hunt down the ingredients, and basically devote an entire weekend to it. And during those phases, it feels incredibly rewarding, not just because of the end result, but because the process itself is almost meditative — you're focused, you're using your hands, you're following a sequence that demands just enough attention to keep you present. But then, just as quickly, the enthusiasm fades, and I'll fall back into my usual rotation of maybe four or five reliable meals. So I suppose what keeps me coming back isn't a genuine ambition to become a better cook, but more the occasional craving for that sense of discovery — the novelty of turning raw materials into something you've never made before. It's the act of learning, really, not the cooking per se.

### 为什么这是 C2（词汇未超雅思范围）

**核心逻辑：C2 的标志不是"说得好听"，而是"把一个简单的 yes/no 问题，回答出真实的心理纹理"。** 全篇没有超出雅思词汇表的词，但通过以下维度达到 C2：

| 维度 | 体现 | 作用 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **拒绝扁平回答** | 没有直接说 "Yes, I love cooking new dishes because..."，而是先给一个定性：interest comes in waves | 一开始就暗示"事情没那么简单"，为后面的层次展开铺路，体现对自身行为的**反思能力** |
| **场景还原密度** | "stumbling across a recipe video → deciding to master it → researching the technique → hunting down ingredients → devoting an entire weekend" | 五步动词链，不是抽象概括，而是**还原了真实的行为轨迹**，让考官"看见"画面，这是 C2 与 C1 的分水岭——C1 会说 "I spend a lot of time trying new recipes"，C2 让你**亲历**那个过程 |
| **过程价值的独立命名** | "not just because of the end result, but because the process itself is almost meditative" | 大多数考生只会评价"好吃不好吃"，但这里把价值拆成**结果层**和**过程层**，并给过程一个精确的心理标签，这种元认知能力是 C2 的核心特征 |
| **自我修正与诚实** | "But then, just as quickly, the enthusiasm fades"、"fall back into my usual rotation" | C2 说话者不怕暴露自己的矛盾和不一致，反而**主动展示反复无常**，这比一味正面描述要可信得多，也复杂得多 |
| **最终落点的精准区分** | "It's the act of learning, really, not the cooking per se" | 用 "X, not Y per se" 结构把两个容易混淆的概念剥离开，这种**概念辨析能力**是 C2 思维的典型表现 |
| **口语衔接自然度** | "though I should say"、"say,"、"And during those phases"、"So I suppose what keeps me coming back isn't..." | 每个衔接词都在做具体的工作——让步、举例、递进、总结——而非机械填充 |

### 实战缩短版（考试建议 15–20 秒说完）

> Yes, but in phases — I'll suddenly get obsessed with a particular dish, spend a whole weekend perfecting it, and then just as quickly lose interest and go back to my usual meals. What I actually enjoy isn't the cooking itself, but that feeling of discovery when you turn something new into a finished dish.

缩短策略：保留 **波浪式兴趣的主线 + 一个具体场景碎片 + 最后的概念剥离**，砍掉 meditative 的展开和五步动词链的中间步骤，但"学"与"做"的区分依然在。
