Here's a C2-level response in a mini essay style:

---

Almost certainly, yes — though I try not to attach too rigid a timeline to it, because I've learned that the decision to move is rarely as rational as we'd like to believe. My current apartment suits my needs well enough for now: the location is convenient, the rent is manageable, and I've established enough of a routine that daily life runs without friction. But I can already feel a certain restlessness setting in — not because anything is wrong, but precisely because everything is *fine*, and "fine" has a way of quietly becoming a trap if you're not attentive.

The question of where I'd go next is interesting because it pulls in two completely different directions. On one hand, there's a pragmatic case for moving closer to where the industry I'm in is concentrated — better networking, more face-to-face interactions, a denser professional ecosystem. On the other hand, I find myself increasingly drawn to the idea of a smaller city, somewhere with a slower tempo, where the cost of living wouldn't force every life decision through the filter of economic calculation. I haven't resolved that tension yet, and I suspect I won't until something external — a job offer, a relationship, or just the right moment — tips the balance.

So, will I move? Yes. But I think the more honest answer is that I'm already moving, in a psychological sense. The moment you start mentally auditing your current living situation against an imagined alternative, the departure has, in some way, already begun.

---

- **句式多样性**：破折号补充说明、让步从句（*though I try not to*）、原因状语从句嵌套（*not because... but precisely because*）、介词短语前置、虚拟条件暗示、分词结构
