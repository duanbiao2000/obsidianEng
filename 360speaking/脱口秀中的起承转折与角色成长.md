## 编剧思路

**重新设计内核：** 讲述者是一个在外漂泊的中国年轻人 红烧肉 = 与父亲之间沉默关系的唯一语言 喜剧外壳包裹的是一个关于**读懂爱**的成长故事

**起承转合：**

- **起** — 童年记忆，爸爸不善言辞，只会做红烧肉
- **承** — 离家，觉得那碗肉没什么了不起
- **转** — 自己第一次尝试做，才明白那锅肉有多难
- **合** — 读懂了沉默背后的东西

**喜剧策略：** 笑点埋在细节里，情感留在最后三句

---

## 短喜剧：**The Longest Recipe**

My dad was never a man of many words.

Like, _clinically_ few words.

Growing up, our dinner conversations went like this:

"How was school?" "Fine." "Good."

...and then just the sound of chopsticks. 🥢

---

But every Sunday — without fail, without announcement —

the whole apartment filled with this smell.

Soy sauce. Rock sugar. Slow heat.

Braised pork belly. Every. Single. Sunday.

That was his word count for the week.

---

I left home at eighteen.

Big city. New life. Very confident.

Told myself I didn't miss much —

except maybe... okay fine, _that dish._

But I was too proud to admit it, so I just called it "protein" and moved on. 😅

---

Then one winter, broke, homesick, too stubborn to call —

I decided to make it myself.

How hard could it be, right?

It's just pork. And sauce. And time.

---

Three hours later —

the pork was grey. The sauce was bitter. My smoke detector had opinions.

And I was sitting on the kitchen floor eating something that tasted like _regret in a bowl._

---

So I called my dad.

First call in four months.

Didn't say I was homesick. Didn't say I was struggling.

Just said —

"Hey. How do you make the pork thing."

Long pause.

"You have rock sugar?"

"...no."

Another pause.

"Go buy rock sugar. Call me back."

---

And we spent forty minutes on the phone —

him walking me through every step, me writing it down like it was sacred text.

He didn't ask how I was doing. I didn't say I missed him.

But somewhere between _medium heat_ and _don't rush the sauce_ —

I heard everything he never knew how to say. 🥺

---

The dish still wasn't as good as his.

Honestly it wasn't even close.

But I ate the whole thing standing over the stove at midnight —

and for the first time in months,

I felt like I was somewhere warm. 🌿

---

They say some people show love with words.

My dad shows love with pork fat and two hours of patience.

For a long time I thought that wasn't enough.

Now I think —

some recipes take a lifetime to understand.

And the ones worth learning...

always start with a phone call you almost didn't make. ✨
