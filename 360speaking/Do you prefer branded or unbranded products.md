### C2 迷你作文式回答

> I'd love to give you a clean answer to that, but the honest truth is my behaviour is completely inconsistent, and I think that inconsistency itself is probably more interesting than a straightforward preference. Because on paper, I'd say I'm not brand-conscious at all — I'm perfectly happy buying unbranded groceries, basic household items, things where the functional difference is essentially zero. But then I'll catch myself reaching for a branded phone, or wearing a specific label on a jacket, and I have to admit that in those moments, I'm clearly not making a purely rational decision. What I think is really going on is that I use brands as a shortcut in areas where I don't have the expertise to judge quality independently. If I'm buying something technical, or something I don't understand well, a brand acts as a kind of guarantee — maybe not a perfect one, but a psychological safety net at least. And I suspect that's true for most people, even if they don't frame it that way. So it's less a question of "preference" and more a question of context — I don't prefer brands, but I do lean on them when the cost of making a wrong choice feels too high.

### 为什么这是 C2（词汇未超雅思范围）

**核心逻辑：这道题的框架是"二选一"。C2 的做法是**拒绝进入这个框架**，不是在 A 和 B 之间选一个，而是指出"我自以为在选，其实我的选择逻辑根本不统一"，然后揭示这个不统一背后的真实心理机制。**

| 维度 | 体现 | 作用 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **元叙事开场** | "I'd love to give you a clean answer, but the honest truth is my behaviour is completely inconsistent" | 不进入 A/B 选择题，而是先宣告"我的答案不适合你的问题格式"——这种**对问题前提的温和挑战**是 C2 说话者的标志性起手式 |
| **自我暴露作为论证** | "I'll catch myself reaching for a branded phone... and I have to admit" | 不是在分析别人，而是在**当场拆穿自己的虚伪**——"我说我不在意品牌，但我的手出卖了我"，"catch myself" 暗示这个行为是无意识的，自己事后才察觉，这种自我监控的颗粒度远超 C1 |
| **行为归因而非标签化** | "I use brands as a shortcut in areas where I don't have the expertise to judge quality independently" | 不说"我是品牌控"或"我是理性消费者"，而是给行为一个**因果机制**——品牌=信息不足时的决策捷径，这个框架把一个消费偏好问题变成了认知科学问题 |
| **精确的心理隐喻** | "a psychological safety net" | 不说 "it makes me feel safe"（太泛），而是用 "safety net"——安全网是**你希望用不到但知道它在的东西**，暗示品牌消费的本质不是信任，而是**对不信任的对冲**，这个词的选择让一层含义变成了两层 |
| **从个人推及普遍** | "I suspect that's true for most people, even if they don't frame it that way" | C2 不只谈论自己，还能**从个案中提取通用模型**，"don't frame it that way" 暗示大多数人有这个行为但缺乏这个认知，一句话完成了"现象→机制→大众盲区"的三级跳 |
| **结尾的概念降维** | "I don't prefer brands, but I do lean on them when the cost of making a wrong choice feels too high" | "prefer" 暗示主动喜好，"lean on" 暗示被动依赖——用一个动词替换就把整个关系从**情感层面拉到了功能层面**，"cost of making a wrong choice" 则把品牌消费从"面子问题"重新定义为**风险管理问题** |
| **口语控制力** | "on paper,"、"essentially zero"、"maybe not a perfect one, but..." | "on paper" 极其精准地暗示了"理论与实际的反差"——不是"理论上"，而是"写下来看是这样"，三个词替代了一段解释 |

### 实战缩短版（考试建议 15–20 秒说完）

> I'd say I'm not brand-conscious, but honestly, I'm inconsistent. For everyday things I don't care about labels at all, but for things I don't know much about — like electronics — I do lean on brands as a shortcut, because I can't judge the quality myself. So it's not really a preference; it's more of a safety net when the cost of a wrong choice is high.

缩短策略：保留 **"言行不一"的自我暴露 + "品牌=信息不足时的捷径"这个核心机制 + "prefer vs lean on" 的动词区分**，砍掉从个人推及普遍的 meta 层和 "safety net" 的展开，但"不是偏好，是风险管理"这个概念翻转完整保留。

I want to give you a clear answer, but the truth is my behaviour is not consistent at all — and I think that is actually more interesting than a simple answer.

If you ask me on paper, I would say I don't care about brands. I am happy to buy products with no brand name on them — food, daily items, things where the difference between cheap and expensive is basically nothing. But then sometimes I find myself choosing a well-known phone, or wearing a jacket with a famous name on it. In those moments, I have to be honest — I am clearly not making a fully logical decision.

What I think is really happening is this: I use brands as a shortcut in areas where I don't know enough to judge the quality myself. When I am buying something technical, or something I don't understand well, a famous brand feels like a kind of promise — maybe not a perfect one, but at least it makes me feel safer about my choice. And I think most people do the same thing, even if they never say it that way.

So it is less about "preference" and more about the situation. I don't prefer brands in general — but I do rely on them when I feel that making the wrong choice would cost me too much.
