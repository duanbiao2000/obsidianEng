Well, at present I'm pursuing a postgraduate degree in data science, though I'd say the distinction between "student" and "worker" has become increasingly blurred in my case. A substantial portion of my week is devoted to an industry placement, where I'm applying machine learning models to real-world datasets — so in many respects, I already operate in a professional capacity. That said, the academic framework still shapes my daily rhythm: I attend seminars, work towards a dissertation, and am held to the kind of intellectual rigour that you'd associate with formal study rather than employment.

I find this hybrid arrangement genuinely stimulating. The research side satisfies my curiosity — it allows me to grapple with theoretical questions that don't always have immediate practical payoffs — while the placement keeps me anchored to the messy, unpredictable reality of actually deploying solutions in a business context. If anything, each sphere enriches the other: a concept I encounter in a lecture might resurface weeks later as a surprisingly elegant fix for a problem at work, and conversely, the constraints I run into on the job often send me back to the literature with much sharper questions.

So, if forced to choose a label, I'd call myself a student — but one who has one foot firmly planted in the professional world already.

---

### 为什么这个回答达到 C2 水平

- **词汇精准度高**：*blurred distinction, postgraduate degree, substantial portion, intellectual rigour, hybrid arrangement, grapple with, immediate practical payoffs, anchored to, deploying solutions, elegant fix, sharper questions*
- **句式多样性**：包含让步状语从句、定语从句、破折号插入语、分词结构，长短句交替
- **逻辑层次清晰**：第一段界定身份，第二段分析利弊，第三段收束观点 — 典型的 mini essay 三段结构
- **避免套话**：没有使用常见的 *I'm a student at XX University* 这种模板化开头，而是从"学生与工作者的界限模糊"这个角度切入，自然展现思维深度
